About Me

Hi my name is Daniel Toebe, I’m an Cancer. I enjoy long windy walks in the desert… Ha ha, but seriously…

It has taken me a while to come up with this page. Funny since most of my professional career I’ve been a salesman, and this page is meant to sell myself, of sorts. While sales was fun, I did meet interesting people, but my true passion is software development.

Programming

Software development is one of those things that constantly keeps you on your toes, challenges you in different ways about every minute. You can approach the same problem in so many different ways with different tool sets.

As you can see I am a very passionate, and self taught programmer. I am proficient in quite a few languages and frameworks, such as: Golang, NodeJS/ES6, Electron, JavaScript, the React framework, Python, Flask, Kivy, C++, Qt frameworks, Java, and Rust. Lately in my free time I have been working with Scala.

Linux

On the other side of things I love working in a Linux environment. I use many various distros for particular needs. For my daily desktop I will use Arch Linux, or OpenSUSE Tumbleweed, either with a Plasma 5 desktop, Gnome, or i3wm, but KDE and I go way back. I prefer these distros on my desktop because I like the rolling-release model so I never have to do major version updates, and I like having the latest packages for my dev environments. On the server side I look for the right tool (distro) for the job. Whether it be Debian, Ubuntu, or CentOS. I can mange packages with Apt, Yum, and the newer DNF.

Personal

When I am not glued to my computer I really do love the outdoors. I grew up outside a small town, where my closest neighbors were at least a half a mile way from me. I love to go out and explore. I have seen some of the most remote beautiful places in Arizona, remote ancient ruins, and the sunsets… Wow the Arizona sunsets are amazing.

Well thats about it. If you got this far then you either find me fascinating, or you are really bored. Ha ha.. Well I am looking for new opportunities, and challenges in software engineering.
